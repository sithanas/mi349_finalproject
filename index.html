The Foods of Consulting

A Tale of Expense Reports (and Creativity)

Consulting involves a lot of travel. Travel involves a lot of food. When the opportunity for food is combined with the ability to expense said food, things get...interesting. Below you will find some of the more interesting dietary discoveries from the road more traveled—no McDonald's pictures here!

Sushi / Sashimi Combo

Palmdale, CA — June 2020

Had a pretty decent sushi / sashimi combo platter in California. The rolls were good-not-great—decent, but I've definitely had better, and the same unfortunately goes for the tuna nigiri. The salmon sashimi, however, was excellent—buttery and rich with a nice melt-on-your-tongue finish. Definitely worth the expense report.

King-Sized King Crab

Anchorage, AK — September 2019

When a sales dinner offers you the opportunity to order a two-pound king crab leg you absolutely do not say no. Complete with garlic mashed potatoes, green beans (so you can say you ate something healthy I guess?), and an entire cup of warm drawn butter. Also seen: giant hunk of steak (left) and halibut cheeks (right). Highly recommended—I'd even spend my own money on this if I had to, though after seeing the bill I'm glad I didn't have to.

Alaska-Sized Shade

Anchorage, AK — September 2019

No food on this one—just Alaska reminding everyone who the real biggest state is. I did have the halibut order though (the Texas-sized one) and can attest to it being pretty damn good.

Dim Sum Assortment

San Francisco, CA — August 2019

When you end up in the Bay Area, you take the opportunity to get some excellent Chinese food—so dim sum it is! Soup dumplings to start, followed by everything else we could get our hands on—and done properly, too, with the food brought around on carts. Don't forget the chili oil!

Burger Done Right

Bellevue, NE — May 2019

Generic burgers are a dime a dozen, but truly well prepared patties are something special. This burger had an extra element—a topping of pastrami over the patty. Also your eyes are correct—there's no plate in this picture. They serve the burgers right on the napkin!

Airport Udon

Minneapolis, MN — April 2019

One of the hidden gems of the Minneapolis airport is an excellent Asian restaurant tucked in a hallway near the G terminals. Their sushi is decent, but their real specialties are their noodle dishes—including this excellent udon noodle soup. Definitely check it out on your next long layover.

Skylounge Life

Yet Another Airport — March 2019

No food here—just a quick picture of what it's like to work out of a skylounge during yet another layover. I could not for the life of me tell you which airport this was—they all blur together at a certain port, and every Delta lounge looks like every other Delta lounge—at least in the business center area.

Bulgogi Hot Pots

Minneapolis, MN — March 2019

Tried out a new lounge in the Minneapolis airport and found these adorable individual-sized bulgogis. Pretty decent as it turned out—it didn't last long enough to get a photo of the dish itself!

Sky Salad

Up In The Air — February 2019

This is a decent example of one of the better dishes you can get if / when you get upgraded to First on a Delta flight—a cold chicken noodle salad with a pretty solid cookie for dessert. Delta regional jets can't serve hot food (no oven in the galley) so you get salads or sandwiches, and this salad was pretty good—not restaurant quality, but defintiely an improvement over the bag of pretzels. (I still grabbed the Biscoff though—never gonna pass those up!)

Pheasant Casserole

Kettering, England — January 2018

When your English hotel has pheasant casserole on the menu, you try pheasant casserole for dinner. Pretty good—flavors were solid, the presentation was excellent, but as it would turn out you can shoot game animals and then serve them in restaurants in the UK—picking lead shot out of the dish was a little bit of a turn-off.

Pigeon Hearts (and Livers) on Toast

Molesworth, England — January 2018

Yes, you absolutely read that right—this was an appetizer at one of the stranger restaurants I've ever been to. The dish was "Pigeon Hearts and Livers with Lentils on Toast" and we had to try it—because when else are you going to have an opportunity to eat pigeon? They were very chewy. I'd also like to take a moment to point out how many hearts are on this plate—let's pause and have a moment of silence for the minimum of 8 pigeons that died to make this dish possible.

Scallops and Black Pudding

Molesworth, England — January 2018

Pretty delicious appetizer—the soft texture of the scallops went well with the crisped black pudding. Admittedly, those of you who know what black pudding is may not especially like the dish—but if you don't mind the idea of eating what is effectively congealed blood, you'll find this to be quite delicious. As a fan of black sausage, I didn't mind one bit.
